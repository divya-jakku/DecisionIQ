FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about DecisionIQ and how it works under the hood.

Does DecisionIQ tell me what decision to make?

▼
No — and intentionally so. DecisionIQ evaluates the conditions under which you're making a decision, not the decision itself. It answers: "Are you in a good state to make this call right now?" You stay in full control of the actual choice.

How accurate is the ML model?

▼
The Random Forest model achieves ~84% accuracy on a held-out test set. It was trained on 1,200 synthetic scenarios with realistic correlations — stress and time pressure co-vary naturally, experienced people tend to be more confident, and overconfident novices are flagged separately with overconfidence bias rules.

What is the Cognitive Bias Detector?

▼
The system checks for 5 named decision biases from your input pattern: Overconfidence (high confidence, low experience), Tunnel Vision (high pressure + few alternatives), Information Illusion (confident but under-informed), Stress Panic (extreme stress + few alternatives), and Analysis Paralysis (max alternatives + very low confidence). Each detected bias unlocks as a dramatic named badge.

Does it use paid APIs or external services?

▼
No. DecisionIQ is entirely powered by a Random Forest ML model built with scikit-learn (free, open-source). All explanations, bias detections, goal indicators, and advice are rule-based logic — no OpenAI, no paid APIs, no rate limits. The backend runs fully on your local machine.

What is the Reversibility Indicator?

▼
One question in the wizard asks: "Is this decision reversible?" If you're in high-risk territory but the decision is reversible, the system labels it "High Risk (Recoverable)" — cautious but not alarming. If it's irreversible, you get a critical warning: "Treat this as one category riskier." This is backed by actual decision theory.

Who is this built for?

▼
Three audiences: Students choosing projects or career paths, Professionals evaluating business or investment decisions, and Individuals navigating important personal choices. If you've ever wondered "am I in the right headspace for this?" — this tool gives a data-backed answer.
